Designed for Students

Aviation is exciting, but structured and regulated. We help students understand what it truly demands.

Pilot Training

Learn how pilot training actually works — from Private Pilot Licence (PPL) to Commercial Pilot Licence (CPL). We explain flight hours, DGCA / EASA / FAA licensing, medical requirements, costs, and realistic timelines.

  • Students aiming for airline or commercial flying careers
  • Those who meet or can work towards aviation medical standards
  • Students ready for long-term, disciplined training
  • Individuals open to training in India or abroad

Aviation Management

Understand the business and operations side of aviation including airport operations, airline management, safety management systems, ground handling, and regulatory frameworks.

  • Students interested in aviation but not in flying aircraft
  • Those inclined towards planning, coordination, and leadership roles
  • Aspirants looking for careers in airports or airline operations
  • Students seeking aviation exposure without flight training costs

Engineering & Technical Roles

Explore technical aviation careers such as aircraft maintenance engineering, avionics, and safety-critical technical roles that keep aircraft airworthy.

  • Students with interest in engineering, mechanics, or electronics
  • Those who prefer hands-on, technical work environments
  • Aspirants aiming for licensed maintenance or avionics roles
  • Individuals interested in safety-focused aviation careers

Career Pathways

Aviation careers develop in stages. Click each stage to understand what it involves and how different roles open up as you progress.

1

Training Phase

This is where students enter aviation through structured education such as pilot training, aviation management programs, or technical courses. Foundations are built during this phase.
2

Licensing & Certification

Completion of required examinations, certifications, and medical assessments under authorities like DGCA, EASA, or FAA depending on the chosen pathway.
3

Career Entry

Entry-level professional roles such as First Officer, operations executive, or maintenance engineer based on training and licensing.
4

Career Growth

Long-term progression into senior pilot positions, leadership roles, specialized technical certifications, or emerging aviation sectors.
